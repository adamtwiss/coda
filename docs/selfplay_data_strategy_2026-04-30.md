# Self-Play Training Data — Strategy Analysis (2026-04-30)

## Question

Coda currently trains on LC0-generated T80 binpacks (the data Stockfish uses). Adam ran one experiment: 3B positions of pure V5 self-play, training V9 → **−20 Elo** vs T80 of similar size. Is iterative self-play a viable long-term path? When and how?

## Headline finding from the cross-engine survey

**Coda's −20 Elo result is not anomalous — it matches the published self-play literature almost exactly.** Viridithas's `viri52` (the most directly comparable public experiment) reports **−16.4 Elo** for 450M fresh self-play positions used as a replacement for the established corpus, with shuffling enabled. `viri51` (same data, no shuffling) was **−68.4 Elo**. Coda's result sits squarely in the expected band.

Source: `engines/viridithas/notes/networkhistory.txt:148-158`.

The published evidence rules out "one iteration of self-play at parity scale, replacing T80" as a viable path. **Every successful self-play recipe across 17 surveyed engines does one or more of:** (a) mix self-play *into* the existing corpus, (b) re-evaluate the existing corpus with a stronger net, (c) use a *bigger relabelling teacher* than the runtime net, or (d) bootstrap from random weights and iterate continuously.

## Why the V5→V9 experiment was a stronger headwind than "self-play vs T80"

Two compounding gaps the experiment confounds:

1. **Teacher eval quality gap.** LC0 in T80 is super-human (~3700-4000+). Coda V5 at d7-8 is ~3000-3200. ~800 Elo of eval quality. Filtering more positions doesn't recover that.

2. **V5→V9 architecture mismatch.** V9 has threat-aware features V5 doesn't. Training V9 on V5 evals teaches the threat-aware student to imitate the threat-blind teacher's blindspots. The features V9 *exists to learn* are exactly what V5 underweights.

The V5→V9 result over-states the cost of *same-architecture* self-play. A clean A/B would be V9-self-play vs T80, both at matched scale. Cosmo's experiments on viri52/53 are roughly that clean — and still negative at single-iteration replacement.

## The four recipes that actually work

Drawn from the cross-engine survey of 17 engines (`engines/{Stockfish, Obsidian, Alexandria, Quanticade, Stormphrax, viridithas, PlentyChess, Caissa, Tarnished, integral, Reckless, Halogen, CloverEngine, Astra, blackmarlin, Ethereal, …}`):

### Recipe A — Random-weight RL bootstrap (Stormphrax, Halogen)

`engines/Stormphrax/src/datagen/datagen.cpp:76-90` — `kDatagenSoftNodeLimit = 24000`, `kDatagenHardNodeLimit = 8388608`, 8 + (rng & 1) random plies, verification search at depth 10 (1M nodes) rejecting `|score| > 500cp`, win adjudication `|normScore| > 1250cp` for 5 plies, draw adjudication `plyFromStart >= 70 AND |normScore| < 10cp` for 10 plies. DFRC supported.

Stormphrax explicitly disclaims third-party data ("will never use data generated by a third party") and has run 70+ iterations off random init. Halogen does TD-lambda from random + supervised SL fine-tune.

**For Coda: not applicable.** This is a multi-year commitment that throws away LC0 quality. Only viable if you want a from-scratch ideologically-pure RL net.

### Recipe B — Distillation with a bigger relabelling teacher (Tarnished)

`engines/Tarnished/README.md:46-48`:
> 5000/20000 soft nodes for self play / 8 random plies for opening / **Relabeled with `(768x8hm→4096)x2→(96→192→192→1)x8` network** / ~22b positions

Two-stage: positions are generated by self-play with the runtime net, but score targets are re-labelled with a much wider teacher (4096 accumulator, 96→192→192 hidden chain). This sidesteps the "we can't beat our teacher" ceiling because the *labels* come from a stronger evaluator than the runtime net produces.

**For Coda: highly actionable.** Train one wide V9 (e.g. 2048 accumulator + bigger hidden, 1.5-2× normal training cost), then use it ONLY for relabelling. The wide teacher doesn't need to be fast — only accurate. This is the highest-ceiling option.

### Recipe C — Re-evaluate the corpus + small fresh injections (Viridithas)

The cleanest published log of a successful self-play strategy. `engines/viridithas/notes/networkhistory.txt`:

| Net | Action | Elo Δ | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| viri12 | viri11 data re-evaluated with viri11 net | **+50** | `:21-22` |
| viri14 | viri13 data re-evaluated, deduplicated | **+25** | `:27-28` |
| viri18 | viri17 data re-evaluated with viri17 | **+23.9** | `:39-40` |
| viri21 | 320K fresh games **mixed into** corpus | **+7.6** | `:48-49` |
| viri30, viri35, etc. | Inject ~250M-1B fresh on top, repeat | **+4 to +8 per cycle** | `:80-82, 94-96` |
| viri53 | viri49 dataset + 450M RL FENs (1.35B total) | **+6.95** | `:160-164` |

Key invariants:
- **Re-evaluate only when scoring with a STRONGER net** (viri22 with HCE = −10.5, viri23 with weaker net = −23.5; `:52-56`).
- **Mix, don't replace.** viri20 replaced with 320K self-play → −106 Elo; viri21 same data mixed in → +7.6 (`:45-49`).
- **Shuffle discipline.** viri51 (no shuffle) −68.4; viri52 (same data, shuffled) −16.4. **Shuffling alone = +50 Elo** (`:148-158`).
- **Filtering bugs invisible at small scale, devastating at large.** viri37/38 lost 87.6 Elo to a noisy-move filter bug that didn't show at small scale (`:101-111`).

**For Coda: this is the cheapest positive-Elo recipe.** Re-evaluating T80 positions with our current V9 net (which is stronger on Coda's specific position distribution than LC0 is on those positions) is well-documented as +25 to +50 Elo at small scale, then +4 to +8 per cycle.

### Recipe D — Per-iteration self-play, opening-book + Syzygy seeded (Integral)

`engines/integral/README.md:21-26`:
> Each self-play game starts with 3-4 randomly selected moves off **UHO_Lichess_4852_v1** ... 5-man Syzygy ... used to guide the data generation search. The first iteration was trained on data from version 4 [HCE]. **Each iteration of Integral's neural network since then has been trained on a fresh dataset using the prior network.**

Continuous chain. Quanticade is mid-transition to this pattern. Caissa explicitly purges old games each generation (`Caissa/README.md:289`).

**For Coda: viable as a longer-term replacement strategy** if/when we plateau on T80, but expensive (every iteration is a fresh corpus). The opening-book seed is critical for diversity — Coda's current 4-9 random plies is much weaker than UHO_Lichess_4852_v1's 8000+ curated openings.

## Empirical Elo deltas — the concrete record

Single-replacement at parity scale (worst pattern):
- **Coda (V5→V9, 3B): −20 Elo** ← Adam's experiment
- **viri52 (450M, shuffled): −16.4 Elo**
- viri29 (960K, no Lichess Elite): ~−60 Elo
- viri51 (450M, no shuffle): −68.4 Elo

Mixing self-play with corpus:
- viri21 (320K mixed in): +7.6
- viri53 (450M RL added to viri49 corpus, 1.35B total): +6.95

Re-evaluating corpus with stronger net:
- viri12: +50
- viri14: +25
- viri18: +23.9

Distilled smaller-arch student:
- viri's `alembic` (distilled from `sensei`): −12.7 / −26 (`:846-862`). **Distillation only helps when student ≥ teacher size; otherwise the teacher's gain is lost.**

**No engine in the survey reports beating LC0/T80 by replacing it in a single step.** The closest analogue is Stormphrax's pure-RL-from-random achievement (currently 119 Elo on the RR vs Coda 62) — but that took 70+ iterations and started from random.

## Where Coda actually sits

We're at +62 Elo on the RR (rank 21). Reckless and SF (both T80-trained) are at +230. **There are 170 Elo of demonstrated headroom on the data we already have** — Reckless proves you can reach top tier on T80. Until we close most of that gap with our current data source, switching primary data is premature.

This matches the "consensus H0 = dig deeper" lesson in CLAUDE.md: the right reaction to "we trained on T80 and we're 170 Elo behind another T80 engine" is to fix Coda's training/architecture/inference, not to swap data sources. The 2026-04-25 cross-engine doc lists +30-60 Elo of structural fixes still on the table.

## Recommendation matrix

**Don't:**
- Replace T80 with single-iteration self-play at parity scale. Empirically negative across the board.
- Re-evaluate T80 with a *weaker* net (viri22/23 lost Elo).
- Skip shuffling. Worth ~+50 Elo by itself.
- Train a smaller-arch distilled student of a wider teacher (viri's alembic lost 12-26 Elo).

**Cheap, near-term, positive-EV:**

1. **Re-evaluate a slice of T80 with the current best V9 net** (Recipe C). Start with 1B positions, re-score, retrain, measure delta. Published precedent: +25 to +50 Elo at small scale, +4 to +8 per cycle thereafter. Compute: ~hours of CPU per iteration. **This is the highest-ROI experiment in this whole space.** If positive at 1B, expand and iterate.

2. **Mix small fresh V9 self-play injections into T80** (Recipe C variant). 250M-1B positions per cycle, *added* not replacing. Published +4 to +8 per cycle.

**Medium-term, higher ceiling:**

3. **Train a wide V9 teacher (Tarnished pattern, Recipe B).** Build one big-arch net (2048 acc, 96→192→192 hidden, 1.5-2× normal training cost), use ONLY for relabelling — not for inference. Then re-evaluate the existing T80 corpus. The widest version of Recipe C: highest theoretical ceiling, requires one wide-net training run. Plan for this once Coda has closed >80 Elo on the current top-10 gap.

**Long-term, only if we plateau on T80 and Recipes B/C are saturated:**

4. **Continuous-chain self-play (Recipe D, Integral pattern).** Per-iteration fresh corpus seeded from UHO_Lichess_4852_v1 + Syzygy, growing depth (start d8, scale toward d12+). Plan for 5-10 iterations before judging the curve. Compute-heavy.

## Concrete next experiments

**E1. T80 re-evaluation pilot.** Re-score the latest 100M T80 positions with current V9 prod net. Replace those scores in the binpack. Train a clone V9 on the modified corpus. SPRT vs current trunk. **Expected: +5 to +25 Elo** if the recipe works for us; H0 if our V9 evaluator is *not* stronger than LC0 on that specific position distribution. This experiment also tells you whether to invest in the wider-teacher recipe (B).

**E2. V9 same-arch self-play pilot, mixed not replaced.** Generate 500M positions of V9 self-play at 25K-node soft / 1M-node hard limits (Stormphrax-shape config), 8 random plies, win adjudication |score|>1500 for 5 plies, draw |score|<10 for 10 plies + plyFromStart>70. Mix into the latest T80 corpus. Train a clone V9. SPRT vs current trunk. **Expected: 0 to +8 Elo** — published precedent is +4 to +8 per cycle when mixed.

**E3. Wide-teacher A/B (longer term).** Train a 1.5-2× wider V9 net for relabelling only. Use it to re-score 1B T80 positions. Compare against E1 result. Whichever wins is the path to invest in.

## Methodology notes for any self-play work

If we go any self-play direction, these are non-negotiable:

- **Shuffle every dataset before training.** Worth ~50 Elo per viri51/52.
- **Filter quality gating.** Verify every pos meets the training quiet-filter (per CLAUDE.md: ply ≥ 16, no checks, no captures, no tactical moves) BEFORE assuming the dataset is usable. viri37/38 lost 87.6 Elo to a noisy-move filter bug.
- **Diversity injection.** 4-9 random plies (Coda current) is the floor. Stormphrax uses 8-9; Tarnished 8; Integral seeds from UHO_Lichess_4852_v1 (8000+ openings) + 3-4 random plies. Coda should adopt UHO openings before any meaningful self-play scale.
- **Adjudication.** Stormphrax-shape: win `|score|>1250` for 5 plies, draw `plyFromStart>=70 AND |score|<10` for 10 plies. Saves 30-60% of generation time on already-decided games.
- **Per-iteration Elo measurement.** No engine in the survey blindly trusts that more iterations = more Elo. Every generation is SPRT'd or RR'd against the prior.

## Why the V5→V9 experiment was informative anyway

It told us:
- One iteration of pure self-play replacement is not viable for Coda (consistent with the published literature).
- Cross-architecture distillation (V5 evals → V9 student) carries an extra penalty beyond same-arch self-play, which the data alone can't quantify but is bounded by the alembic-style result (12-26 Elo penalty).

What it didn't tell us:
- Whether *same-architecture* V9 self-play, mixed into T80 (Recipe C variant), would land. Likely +4 to +8 per cycle by analogy.
- Whether re-evaluating T80 with current V9 (Recipe C primary) would land. Likely +5 to +25 at first cycle by analogy.
- Whether a wide-teacher relabelled corpus (Recipe B) would land. The 230-Elo Tarnished result is the proof point; ceiling is high.
